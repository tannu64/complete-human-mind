# The Complete Human Mind: A Comprehensive Biological Reference

## Introduction

The human brain is the most complex known structure in the universe. Weighing approximately 1.4 kilograms and comprising roughly 86 billion neurons interconnected by an estimated 100–150 trillion synapses, it orchestrates every aspect of human experience — from autonomic regulation of heartbeat and respiration to the emergent phenomena of consciousness, language, and abstract thought.

This reference compiles the current state of neuroscientific knowledge into a structured, textbook-depth resource. Each chapter is a self-contained document covering a major domain of brain biology, with cross-references to related chapters where relevant.

## Key Facts at a Glance

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Average brain weight | ~1,400 g (adult) |
| Number of neurons | ~86 billion |
| Number of glial cells | ~85 billion |
| Number of synapses | ~100–150 trillion |
| Energy consumption | ~20% of body's total (at rest) |
| Cerebral blood flow | ~750 mL/min (~15% of cardiac output) |
| Oxygen consumption | ~20% of body's total |
| Length of myelinated nerve fibers | ~150,000–180,000 km |
| Speed of fastest signals | ~120 m/s (myelinated motor neurons) |
| Neurotransmitter types identified | >100 |

## How to Use This Reference

Each chapter is designed to be read independently. However, the recommended reading order follows the table of contents — progressing from structural foundations through cellular mechanisms, systems-level processing, higher cognitive functions, and finally lifespan changes and cutting-edge research.

Cross-references between chapters are provided as markdown links. Terms introduced in one chapter and elaborated in another are noted with "(see Chapter X)" annotations.
